Our Story

Why Lintelos Exists

A name built on structure

carry the load + open the space = time to design

A lintel is the beam above a door or window — it carries the weight so the opening beneath it can exist. Lintelos works the same way: we carry the load that slows a firm down — the repetitive tasks, the clunky handoffs, the operational drag — so architects have the room, and the time, to design.

Architecture firms run on software that was never built for how they actually work. The result is friction everywhere — hours lost to repetitive production tasks, information stuck between people and tools, and operational work that pulls architects away from design. Lintelos builds the tools and systems that close those gaps.

That might mean automating a repetitive process, connecting tools that don't talk to each other, building a database or a scheduling system, or bringing AI in where it earns its place — always built around your firm's standards and the way your team already works, not a generic template. The point isn't technology for its own sake. It's cleaner, faster, less frustrating work — and more time for the parts that matter.

How We Work

Principles that guide every build

01

Built Around Your Workflow

We start with how your firm actually works — your standards, your templates, your process — and build tools that fit it. We don't force your team into someone else's system.

02

Automation, Not Autopilot

We automate the repetitive work, not the decisions. The tools handle the busywork; your architects stay in control of the design. Human-in-the-loop by default.

03

Results You Can Feel

Every tool earns its place by making the work measurably better: hours saved, fewer errors, cleaner output. If it doesn't move the needle, we don't ship it.
